Pulse lets you visualize your TDD flow right from Eclipse

Coding Kata practices

Pulse is useful when you want to practice your "red to green" time and you are focusing at taking the smallest step to make that test pass.

Refactoring legacy fun

Because we enjoy extracting, inlining, and drawing new interfaces. Pulse can shows us our best moves.

"Real world" projects

Sometimes we do work on those projects. Those that are based in the real world, you know? Pulse is guaranteed to work in those parallel universes as well :)

Show off

Save and export your sessions, they are forever yours to keep. You could always remember those good old days when you and Bob ran 20 times the tests and refactored all that code in only 25 minutes.

Anatomy of a session

Pulse plugs into your testing and refactoring activity to show you a real time chart of your coding session.

The time line shrinks to display all the activities so you can get the big picture.

Installing the plugin

Installing from Eclipse

Go to Help > Install New Software...

Click on Add Site and enter Pulse as the name and http://www.happyprog.com/pulse/update as the location.

A new feature called "Pulse" should show up in the main install area. Select it and continue with the installation.

Minimum Requirements

Pulse requires Eclipse Helios (3.6) or greater to run.
